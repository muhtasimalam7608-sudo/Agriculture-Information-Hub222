Pest Control Strategies

Learn advanced Integrated Pest Management (IPM) techniques to protect your crops naturally and effectively.

Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

Combining biological, physical, and chemical tools in a way that minimizes economic, health, and environmental risks.

Biological Controls

Using natural enemies like predators (ladybugs), parasites, and pathogens to control pest populations without chemicals.

  • • Beneficial Insects
  • • Microbial Pesticides

Organic Remedies

Utilizing neem oil, garlic sprays, and crop rotation to deter pests while maintaining the organic integrity of your farm.

  • • Botanical Sprays
  • • Crop Rotation

Early Detection

Regular field scouting and pheromone traps to identify infestations before they reach an economic injury level.

  • • Field Scouting
  • • Pheromone Traps

Tips for Effective Treatment

1

Identify the Pest Correctly

Not all insects are harmful. Identify your enemy before choosing a treatment.

2

Spot Treatment

Instead of spraying the whole field, target only the infested areas to save costs.

3

Proper Timing

Apply treatments during the pest's most vulnerable life stage (usually early larval stages).
